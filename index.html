Introduction

This Privacy Policy describes how Sky VIP (“we,” “us,” or “our”) collects, uses, stores, and shares information when you use our mobile application Sky VIP (the “App”) and related services (together, the “Services”). By using the Services, you agree to this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the App.

We are committed to protecting your privacy and handling your data in line with applicable laws, including where relevant the EU/UK General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) as amended by the CPRA, and Google Play’s developer policies.

Data controller

The data controller responsible for your personal information is the operator of Sky VIP. For questions, contact us using the details in Contact us below. If applicable law requires a legal entity name or postal address, that information appears in the same section.

Information we collect

Depending on how you use Sky VIP, we may collect the following categories of information:

Information you provide directly

  • Account and profile data: such as display name, username, email address, phone number, password or authentication credentials (stored securely), profile photo, bio, or other content you choose to submit.
  • Communications: messages you send to us (e.g. support requests), feedback, survey responses, or user-generated content you post in the App.
  • Payments (if applicable): billing-related information processed by payment providers; we typically do not store full payment card numbers on our servers.

Information collected automatically

  • Device and technical data: device type, operating system version, unique device identifiers, mobile advertising ID (where permitted and if used), language, time zone, and app version.
  • Usage data: features used, session duration, crash logs, diagnostics, in-app events, and general interaction patterns to improve stability and experience.
  • Network data: IP address and general location derived from IP (e.g. country or region) for security, analytics, and compliance.

Information from third parties

  • If you sign in with a third party (e.g. Google, Apple, or another social login), we may receive basic profile information that provider shares with us according to your settings and their policies.
  • We may receive fraud-prevention or integrity signals from app stores or security partners.

Sensitive categories: We do not intend to collect special categories of data (such as health, racial or ethnic origin, or political opinions) unless the App explicitly requests them for a stated feature and we obtain any required consent or legal basis. If you believe we have received such data by mistake, contact us and we will delete it where required by law.

How we use your information

We use the information above for purposes including:

  • Providing, operating, and maintaining the App and its features (including VIP or subscription features, if offered).
  • Creating and managing your account and authenticating you.
  • Processing transactions, subscriptions, or in-app purchases where applicable.
  • Improving the App, developing new features, and conducting analytics (including aggregated or de-identified statistics).
  • Communicating with you about the Services, updates, security alerts, and support.
  • Detecting, preventing, and addressing fraud, abuse, security issues, and violations of our terms.
  • Complying with legal obligations and responding to lawful requests from public authorities.
  • Sending marketing communications only where permitted by law and, where required, with your consent (you may opt out as described in communications or by contacting us).

Legal basis (EEA, UK, and similar jurisdictions)

Where GDPR or similar laws apply, we rely on one or more of the following legal bases:

  • Contract: processing necessary to provide the Services you request.
  • Legitimate interests: such as improving the App, security, fraud prevention, and analytics, balanced against your rights.
  • Consent: where we ask for consent (e.g. certain notifications, marketing, or optional analytics), you may withdraw consent at any time without affecting prior lawful processing.
  • Legal obligation: where we must process data to comply with the law.

Sharing and disclosure

We may share information in these situations:

  • Service providers: vendors who assist us (e.g. hosting, analytics, crash reporting, customer support tools, email delivery) under contracts that require them to protect your data and use it only for our instructions.
  • Legal and safety: when we believe disclosure is required by law, regulation, legal process, or governmental request, or to protect the rights, property, or safety of us, our users, or others.
  • Business transfers: in connection with a merger, acquisition, financing, or sale of assets, subject to appropriate safeguards and notice where required.
  • With your direction: when you explicitly ask us to share information or connect third-party integrations you authorize.

We do not sell your personal information for money. Where “sale” or “sharing” is defined broadly under U.S. state laws (e.g. for cross-context behavioral advertising), see the California section and ensure your Google Play Data safety declarations match your practices.

Third-party services

The App may include links to third-party sites or integrate SDKs (for example analytics, advertising, authentication, or payment processing). Those third parties have their own privacy policies. We encourage you to read them. We are not responsible for their practices beyond what our agreements require. Specific providers in use are those disclosed in the app listing and in-app notices where required.

Data retention

We retain personal information only as long as necessary for the purposes described in this policy, unless a longer period is required or permitted by law (e.g. tax, accounting, or dispute resolution). When data is no longer needed, we delete or anonymize it in accordance with our retention schedules. Account data is typically retained while your account is active and for a reasonable period afterward to resolve disputes or enforce agreements.

Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures designed to protect your information against unauthorized access, loss, or alteration. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure; we cannot guarantee absolute security. Please use a strong password and keep your credentials confidential.

Your rights

Depending on where you live, you may have the right to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you.
  • Correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Delete your personal information, subject to legal exceptions.
  • Restrict or object to certain processing (including direct marketing).
  • Data portability — receive a copy of data you provided in a structured, commonly used format where technically feasible.
  • Withdraw consent where processing is based on consent.
  • Lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority in your country or region.

To exercise these rights, contact us at the email in Contact us. We may need to verify your identity before responding. You may also be able to delete your account or certain data from within the App settings, if available.

Children’s privacy

Sky VIP is not directed to children under the age required by applicable law for collecting personal data without parental consent (for example 13 in the United States under COPPA, or a higher age in some regions). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children below that age. If you believe we have collected such information, please contact us and we will take steps to delete it promptly. Parents and guardians should supervise minors’ use of the App where appropriate. If the App is offered to children or mixed audiences, we comply with applicable requirements for those audiences, including app store policies that apply to child-directed services.

International transfers

We may process and store information in countries other than your own. Where we transfer personal data from the EEA, UK, or Switzerland to countries not deemed adequate by regulators, we use appropriate safeguards such as standard contractual clauses approved by the European Commission or other lawful mechanisms, as required.

California residents (CCPA / CPRA)

If you are a California resident, you may have additional rights under the CCPA/CPRA, including to know categories of personal information collected, purposes of use, categories of third parties with whom we share information, and in some cases to correct or delete personal information and to opt out of “sale” or “sharing” of personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising, if applicable.

We do not discriminate against you for exercising these rights. To submit a request, use the contact details below. We will verify your request as required by law.
